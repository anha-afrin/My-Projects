17 Strategies of SDG

    • End poverty in all its forms everywhere, ensuring that everyone has access to basic resources and services.

    • Achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture to ensure everyone has enough nutritious food.

    • Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages, aiming to reduce mortality rates and improve health services.

    • Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all, focusing on literacy, numeracy, and skills development.

    • Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls, eliminating discrimination and violence against them.

    • Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all, improving water quality and access to safe drinking water.

    • Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all, increasing the share of renewable energy.

    • Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all.

    • Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation to support economic development and human well-being.

    • Reduce inequality within and among countries, ensuring equal opportunities and reducing disparities in income, education, and health.

    • Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable, improving urban planning and management.

    • Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns, reducing waste and promoting resource efficiency.

    • Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts, implementing strategies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and adapt to climate-related risks.

    • Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources, protecting marine ecosystems and reducing pollution.

    • Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, manage forests sustainably, combat desertification, and halt biodiversity loss.

    • Promote peaceful and inclusive societies, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable institutions at all levels.

    • Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development, enhancing cooperation between governments, the private sector, and civil society.

What is actually Sustainabilty?

Sustainability refers to the ability to meet the current needs of society without compromising the future. In 1987, the United Nations' Brundtland Commission defined sustainability as, “meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.”
